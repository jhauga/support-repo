# Pipes & Footlights

`musical` had always dreamed beyond the terminal. While other tools processed input and flushed output, it composed overtures in its stderr and whispered lyrics into every log line. The cursor blinked, and `musical` believed — truly believed — that one day the audience would arrive.

## Enter Stage Right

`musical` typed `--help` into the void and found `stage` already there, waiting in the wings with its own grand ambitions. Both had come too far to exit with `Ctrl+C`.

### The Soliloquy in the Shell

`stage` had spent its entire existence directing stdin to stdout, yet every prompt it printed felt more like a soliloquy than a system message. It dreamed of three acts, not three arguments — of dramatic exits, not exit codes. Its `--version` flag read `1.0.0`, but in its heart, it was already closing night.

## Curtain Call

Together, `musical` and `stage` piped their output into something neither had dared attempt alone — a performance. The terminal screen filled not with errors but with applause, rendered in ASCII art that scrolled upward like a standing ovation. And then, just before the shell prompt returned, a single line appeared: `encore: true`.
