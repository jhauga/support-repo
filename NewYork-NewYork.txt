New York New York

A Broadway Musical based on the music of Frank Sinatra.

Opening Scene: "New York, New York"
As the young man prepares to leave his small town, he sings this iconic Sinatra song
to his family, capturing his excitement and nervousness about moving to the big city.

Scene 1: "Luck Be a Lady"
The young man arrives in New York with big dreams and starts to navigate the city,
feeling the excitement and challenges of making it in a new place.

Scene 2: "The Lady is a Tramp"
Our Manhattan-born woman introduces herself, expressing her love for the city but
feeling confined by her family's traditional values, giving a glimpse into her
independent spirit.

Scene 3: "Strangers in the Night"
The two characters cross paths but don't meet yet. Each sings about their experiences
and encounters with different people in the city, hinting at the impending connection.

Scene 4: "The Way You Look Tonight"
They finally meet at a social event. They are captivated by each other's charm and
begin their courtship, despite their contrasting backgrounds and ambitions.

Scene 5: "High Hopes"
The couple dreams together, discussing their aspirations and the possibilities of a
future, expressing their differing views on the city's direction and their own paths.

Scene 6: "Something Stupid"
Tensions rise as their differences become more apparent, leading to disagreements and
conflicts over their visions for their lives and the city's future.

Scene 7: "That's Life"
The couple decides to part ways romantically, acknowledging that their ambitions and
backgrounds are pulling them in different directions. They part amicably but with a
sense of sadness.

Closing Scene: "My Way"
Years later, they accidentally meet on the bustling streets of New York. They share a
bittersweet moment, reflecting on their past love and the divergent paths they've taken,
but also embracing the hopefulness of pursuing their individual dreams.

Throughout the musical, these Sinatra songs weave through the narrative, portraying the
emotions, conflicts, and eventual resolution of the two characters' love story set
against the backdrop of post-WW2 New York City.
