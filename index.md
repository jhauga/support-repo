# The Last Train

The platform was empty. Rain hammered the metal roof, punctuating the silence with a rhythm that matched her heartbeat: fast, uneven, desperate. She checked her watch again (10:47 PM), then scanned the tracks for any sign of light. Nothing. The ticket clerk had promised the midnight train still ran, but now, standing alone in the downpour's echo, she wasn't so sure. Her bag felt heavier with each passing minute; inside it, everything she owned that still mattered. The job interview was at 8:00 AM, three cities away. Miss it, and the offer vanished. Stay here, and the offer vanished anyway - along with her last chance at starting over.

A whistle cut through the rain. She jerked her head up, heart leaping, and there it was: a single headlight piercing the darkness. The train groaned to a stop, brakes shrieking metal-on-metal. The doors hissed open. She grabbed her bag and stepped forward, then hesitated. What if this was wrong? What if leaving meant losing the one thing she hadn't packed: the faint, stubborn hope that tomorrow might be different? But the conductor was already calling, "All aboard!" and her feet made the choice her mind couldn't. She climbed the stairs. The doors closed behind her with a soft, final click.
